# Monetization Plan

## Core Value Proposition

Pulse turns raw sports data into fan experiences. The commercial angle is straightforward: fans who follow live sports are one of the most engaged and highest-spending audiences on the internet. Pulse sits between them and the game, which creates multiple monetization paths.

---

## Phase 1 — Free + Sponsorship (Hackathon → Launch)

**Ad-supported free tier**  
The app is fully free. Sponsorship slots in the sidebar (already structured for this — the Solana, Superteam, and TxLINE logos sit there now) are swapped for paying sponsors. Sports betting operators, streaming platforms, and sports apparel brands are natural fits.

**Match-sponsored insights**  
Key AI-generated moments in the Hot Feed can carry a sponsor tag: *"This goal brought to you by [Brand]."* Non-intrusive, contextually placed.

Revenue potential: $500–$5,000/month at early scale with sports betting affiliate partners.

---

## Phase 2 — Pulse Pro Subscription

A paid tier ($3–5/month) unlocking:

| Feature | Free | Pro |
|---|---|---|
| Live scores + basic stats | ✅ | ✅ |
| AI Match Pulse | ✅ | ✅ |
| Advanced tactical AI (player-level, formation analysis) | ❌ | ✅ |
| Pre-match predictions | ❌ | ✅ |
| Custom alerts (goal, red card, momentum shift) | ❌ | ✅ |
| Match replay save & share | ❌ | ✅ |
| Ad-free | ❌ | ✅ |
| Multi-match dashboard | ❌ | ✅ |

Revenue potential: At 1,000 paying users × $4/mo = $4,000 MRR.

---

## Phase 3 — Betting Odds Integration

TxLINE's data layer includes consensus betting odds. These can be surfaced inline — "Argentina currently 1.4 to win" — with affiliate links to regulated bookmakers. Affiliate CPA deals in sports betting pay $50–$200 per depositing user.

**Compliance note:** Betting integrations must comply with local gambling regulations. Pulse would operate as a data/affiliate product, not a bookmaker. All integrations would be clearly disclosed.

---

## Phase 4 — B2B Data Product

The MatchEngine + AI narrative layer has standalone value. Media outlets, sports apps, and fantasy platforms could license:
- Real-time AI match summaries via API
- Match Pulse-as-a-service for any TxLINE-covered competition
- White-label second-screen companion widgets

TxLINE already provides the data infrastructure. Pulse adds the AI narrative and UX layer on top.

---

## Why This Is Viable

- **Distribution:** World Cup 2026 has 104 matches and an estimated 5 billion viewers globally. The TAM is massive and the engagement window is known.
- **Retention:** Once fans bookmark a second-screen companion during the tournament, they retain for the next competition (Euros, Champions League, etc.).
- **Defensibility:** The combination of real-time TxLINE data + AI narrative layer + product quality creates a moat that's hard to replicate quickly.
- **Expansion:** The same stack works for any sport TxLINE covers. The World Cup is the proof of concept.
